summary: In Somerset, a woman crossed her cakes to prevent tiny fairies called Vairies, known for their high-heeled shoes, from dancing on them and leaving marks. Similar folklore appears in neighboring Dorset and Hampshire, where these mythical creatures have different names and mischievous roles.
