summary: Eskimo babies in northern British America face a challenging start to life, born in snow huts and subjected to a series of rituals that dictate their names and life customs. If a child dies, there are specific mourning practices that the mother must follow, including wearing a cap, carrying the child's boots, and visiting the grave to offer food.
