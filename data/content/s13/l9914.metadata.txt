summary: Father Marquette recounted legends of the 'piasa bird,' a mythical creature depicted in relief on the bluffs near Alton, Illinois, and described it as a terrifying man-eater preying on local tribes. The vivid description combined animalistic and humanoid features, leading some to speculate whether early encounters with prehistoric creatures inspired such myths.
