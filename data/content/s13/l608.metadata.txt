summary: Ralph Sutherland, a morose and violent man from Leeds in the Catskills, was sentenced to wear a hangman's noose as a reminder of his crime after being accused of dragging his bound servant girl to her death. His sentence was to remain until he turned ninety-nine, but he died in obscurity and solitude, with the noose still around his neck, marking a life haunted by his actions.
