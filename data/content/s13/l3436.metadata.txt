summary: The Delaware Water Gap, known as Minisink, was once a dwelling place for Native American tribes, including Chief Tamanend, who died tragically at New Britain, Pennsylvania. During the Revolutionary era, the Gap was also a site of conflict, where colonial troops pursued and ultimately overcome the notorious Tory, Oran the Hawk, whose remains were discovered after autumn's leaves had fallen.
