summary: In Welsh tradition, graves have been historically adorned with flowers, illustrating the enduring connection between death and floral life. A custom where mourners throw rosemary into graves symbolizes resurrection, contrasting with the ancient Pagan ritual of using cypress to signify the annihilation of the body.
