summary: In Welsh folklore, the Canwyll Corph or Corpse Candle is a popular and picturesque death-omen that appears as a ghostly candlelight, foretelling imminent death. This superstition, with its variant forms like a flambeau or a light from a person's features, is deeply ingrained in Welsh culture, often symbolizing impending death with varying characteristics such as flame color and size.
