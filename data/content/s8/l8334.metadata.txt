summary: In many parts of Pembrokeshire, activities like ball-playing against church walls and dancing in churchyards were traditional Easter and Sabbath customs, often involving the participation and supervision of clergy. These practices, such as storytelling on 'Cnwc y Celwydd' or witnessing the sun's dance on Easter Day, hint at ancient, possibly Druidic origins, merging with Christian traditions over time.
