summary: In West Wales, the practice of 'Ceffyl Pren' involved creating straw effigies of unfaithful spouses and parading them publicly as a form of community-driven retribution. These effigies were ultimately burnt to shame the offenders, a tradition thought to originate from Druidic customs.
