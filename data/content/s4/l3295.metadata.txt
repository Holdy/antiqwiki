summary: Milton Abbey, originally founded by King Athelstan in 938 and rebuilt after a devastating fire in 1309, stands as a historic site in Dorset with a rich blend of architectural styles and a tumultuous history of renovations and preservations. The abbey's extensive history includes its transformation from a monastery for secular canons to a Benedictine abbey, its survival through the Dissolution under Sir John Tregonwell, and subsequent restorations that preserved its historical and spiritual significance.
